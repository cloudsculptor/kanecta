= IT Incident Response
:doctype: article

This procedure covers how the team responds when something goes wrong with the site's technology — outages, security incidents, data issues, or infrastructure failures.

== Severity levels

*P1 — Critical:* Site is down or inaccessible, data breach suspected, account compromise confirmed.
*P2 — High:* Major feature broken, significant performance degradation, suspected but unconfirmed security issue.
*P3 — Low:* Minor feature broken, cosmetic issue, non-urgent infrastructure concern.

== Immediate response (P1)

. The person who discovers the issue alerts the administrator and at least one other volunteer immediately, using the fastest available channel.
. The administrator (or most technically capable available volunteer) begins diagnosis within 30 minutes.
. A brief status note is posted publicly on the site or via the community's communication channels so people know what is happening.
. If a data breach is suspected, no further diagnosis is undertaken until the administrator has isolated the affected systems.

== Diagnosis and resolution

. Document what is known before making changes: what symptoms are visible, what changed recently, what the logs show.
. Make one change at a time and observe the result.
. Keep a running log of actions taken during the incident.

== Data breach procedure

If a breach of personal data is confirmed or strongly suspected:

. Isolate affected systems immediately.
. Notify the Custodian Board within 24 hours.
. Assess what data was affected and how many people are involved.
. If the breach is notifiable under the Privacy Act 2020, notify the Privacy Commissioner and affected individuals promptly. Take legal advice if unsure.
. Publish a transparent account of what happened once the immediate incident is resolved.

== Post-incident review

After any P1 or P2 incident:

. Write a brief incident report covering: what happened, how it was discovered, what was done, and what would prevent it happening again.
. Share the report with all volunteers.
. Implement any preventive measures identified, with a responsible person and a due date.

== Keeping systems documented

* All credentials must be stored in the shared password manager, not in any individual's personal accounts.
* Access credentials must be transferred when a volunteer steps back from a role.
* The administrator maintains a current record of all services, accounts, and renewal dates.
