= Custodian Board Bylaws
:doctype: article

These bylaws govern the formal operation of the Custodian Board. They flow directly from the constitution and are binding on all Board members. They may only be changed through a formal amendment process consistent with the constitution.

== 1. Meetings

=== 1.1 Frequency
The Custodian Board meets at least once per calendar quarter. Additional meetings may be called as needed.

=== 1.2 Calling a meeting
Any Board member may call a meeting by giving at least five working days' written notice to all other members, stating the business to be transacted.

=== 1.3 Quorum
All five members of the Custodian Board must be present for a meeting to proceed. If a member cannot attend, the meeting must be rescheduled. There are no proxy votes.

=== 1.4 Facilitator
At the start of each meeting, members appoint a facilitator by consensus. The role rotates among members. The facilitator has no casting vote and no authority beyond facilitating discussion.

=== 1.5 Remote attendance
Members may attend by video call. Remote attendance counts toward quorum provided the member can hear and participate in all discussion.

== 2. Decision-making

=== 2.1 Consensus required
All Board decisions require the consensus of all five members. A decision is reached when every member present agrees. There is no majority voting and no casting vote.

=== 2.2 Deadlock
If consensus cannot be reached, the matter is adjourned to a subsequent meeting. If consensus still cannot be reached at that subsequent meeting, the matter is referred to a Special General Meeting of Voting Members.

=== 2.3 Out-of-session decisions
In urgent circumstances, decisions may be made by written consensus (email or equivalent) provided all five members respond and all agree. Any out-of-session decision must be recorded in the minutes of the next meeting.

== 3. Minutes and records

=== 3.1 Minutes
A written record must be kept of every meeting, including: who attended, what was discussed, what decisions were made, and any action items with responsible persons and due dates.

=== 3.2 Publication
Minutes must be published to all Voting Members within 14 days of the meeting.

=== 3.3 Retention
All minutes and Board correspondence must be retained for at least seven years and handed over to the incoming Board at the end of each term.

== 4. Financial authorisation

=== 4.1 Routine expenditure
The Treasurer may authorise expenditure up to NZ$500 without Board approval.

=== 4.2 Significant expenditure
Any single expenditure above NZ$500 requires written authorisation from the Treasurer plus at least one Custodian Board member.

=== 4.3 Financial reporting
The Treasurer must provide a written financial report at every quarterly Board meeting. Accounts must be published publicly within 30 days of each quarter end.

== 5. Conflicts of interest

=== 5.1 Declaration
Any Board member with a financial or personal interest in a matter before the Board must declare that interest at the start of discussion.

=== 5.2 Recusal
A member who has declared a conflict must not participate in the discussion or decision on that matter. Their absence does not affect quorum for that item only.

=== 5.3 Recording
All declared conflicts of interest must be recorded in the minutes.

== 6. Vacancies

=== 6.1 Mid-term vacancy
If a Board position becomes vacant between AGMs, the remaining members shall invite a replacement from eligible Voting Members by consensus, to serve until the next AGM.

=== 6.2 Reduced Board
If a vacancy reduces the Board to fewer than three members, a Special General Meeting must be called within 30 days to conduct a supplementary sortition.

== 7. Complaints

=== 7.1 Receiving complaints
The Board must acknowledge any written complaint from a member or volunteer within five working days.

=== 7.2 Process
The Board must give any person the subject of a complaint a fair opportunity to respond before making a determination.

=== 7.3 Confidentiality
Complaints and proceedings are confidential to the parties and the Board, unless the Board determines that publication is in the interests of the Society.
