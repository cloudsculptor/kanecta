= Volunteer Bylaws
:doctype: article

These bylaws set out the formal expectations for volunteers. They are intentionally minimal — volunteers run this organisation and should not be burdened with bureaucracy. The goal is clarity, not control.

== 1. Becoming a volunteer

=== 1.1 Eligibility
Any Voting Member may volunteer. Voting Membership requires a contribution of NZ$5 or more in the past 12 months and acceptance of the Society's kaupapa and code of conduct.

=== 1.2 No formal application
There is no application process. A person becomes a volunteer by expressing availability, agreeing to work in accordance with the kaupapa, and beginning to contribute.

=== 1.3 Roles
Volunteer roles are defined and published by volunteers themselves. Any volunteer may propose a new role or take on existing responsibilities. There is no approval required from the Custodian Board for positive operational decisions.

== 2. Decision-making

=== 2.1 Collective authority
Volunteers collectively hold all positive decision-making authority over the Society's operations — what to build, what to publish, how to organise. No Board approval is required.

=== 2.2 Resolving disagreements
Where volunteers disagree on an operational matter, they resolve it among themselves by discussion and consensus. The Custodian Board does not adjudicate operational disagreements unless a formal complaint is lodged.

=== 2.3 No hierarchy
There is no management hierarchy among volunteers. Influence comes from contribution, expertise, and the trust of other volunteers — not from title or seniority.

== 3. Conduct

=== 3.1 Kaupapa
Volunteers are expected to act in accordance with the Society's kaupapa at all times — in their work, their communications, and their interactions with other members and the community.

=== 3.2 Code of conduct
Volunteers must comply with the Society's code of conduct. Breaches may result in a formal complaint to the Custodian Board.

=== 3.3 Conflicts of interest
Volunteers must not use their position to benefit themselves or any organisation they are connected to. Where a potential conflict arises, it should be declared openly to other volunteers.

== 4. Stepping back and removal

=== 4.1 Stepping back
A volunteer may step back from their role at any time by notifying other volunteers and completing any reasonable handover of work in progress.

=== 4.2 Removal
The Custodian Board may remove a volunteer who has materially breached the kaupapa or code of conduct, after giving them a fair opportunity to respond.
