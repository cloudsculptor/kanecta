= Volunteer Onboarding
:doctype: article

This procedure covers how new volunteers are welcomed and given the access they need to contribute effectively.

== Before they start

. Confirm the person is a current Voting Member (contribution of NZ$5+ in the past 12 months).
. Ask them what they'd like to work on and what skills they're bringing.
. Assign a point of contact from the existing volunteer team — someone who will check in with them in their first few weeks.

== Granting access

Access should be granted based on what the volunteer actually needs. Do not grant more access than required.

. *Site content access* — provided to all volunteers via the site's contributor tools.
. *Code repository access* — granted to volunteers working on the codebase.
. *Shared documents and task tracking* — granted to all volunteers.
. *Social media or communication channels* — granted to volunteers taking on communication responsibilities.
. *Financial systems* — granted only to the Treasurer and with Custodian Board approval.

All access grants must be logged with the volunteer's name, the access level granted, and the date.

== First week

. The point of contact introduces the new volunteer to the rest of the team.
. The new volunteer is given a brief tour of active work in progress and any relevant context.
. The volunteer starts with something small and well-defined — a task they can complete and see the result of quickly.

== Ongoing

. There is no formal probation period. Volunteers are trusted from the start.
. The point of contact checks in informally after two weeks and again after a month.
. If a volunteer is struggling or unsure, the expectation is that they ask — and that the team responds helpfully.

== If a volunteer steps back

. The volunteer notifies the team and agrees a reasonable handover timeline.
. All access is revoked promptly after handover is complete.
. Access revocations are logged.
