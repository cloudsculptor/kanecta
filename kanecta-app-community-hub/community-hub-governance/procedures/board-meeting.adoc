= Custodian Board Meeting
:doctype: article

This procedure covers how a standard Custodian Board meeting is prepared for and run.

== Before the meeting

. *Set the date* — at least five working days before the meeting, confirm the date, time, and location (in person or video call) with all five members.
. *Draft the agenda* — the facilitator (or the member who called the meeting) drafts the agenda and circulates it at least two days before the meeting. A standard agenda includes:
.. Conflicts of interest — any member declares interests relevant to agenda items
.. Minutes of the previous meeting — confirmed as accurate
.. Matters arising from previous minutes
.. Financial report (at quarterly meetings)
.. Substantive items for decision
.. Complaints or formal matters (if any)
.. Correspondence
.. Other business
.. Date of next meeting
. *Prepare papers* — any documents members need to read before the meeting are circulated with the agenda.

== Running the meeting

. Open the meeting and confirm all five members are present.
. Appoint a facilitator by consensus.
. Ask whether any member has a conflict of interest on any agenda item; record any declarations.
. Work through the agenda. The facilitator ensures each member has the opportunity to speak on each item.
. For each decision item, confirm consensus explicitly: "Are we all agreed?" Record the decision.
. Agree action items: for each, record what is to be done, who is responsible, and by when.
. Close the meeting and confirm the date of the next meeting.

== After the meeting

. The facilitator (or an assigned member) writes up the minutes within 48 hours of the meeting.
. Minutes are circulated to all Board members for review.
. Minutes are published to all Voting Members within 14 days.
. Action items are followed up by the responsible persons.
