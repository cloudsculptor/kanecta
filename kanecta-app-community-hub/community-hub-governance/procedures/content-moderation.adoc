= Content Moderation
:doctype: article

This procedure covers how reported or problematic content is handled on the site.

== Receiving a report

. Any community member may report content they believe breaches the site's kaupapa or code of conduct using the reporting tool or by contacting a volunteer directly.
. The volunteer receiving the report acknowledges it within 24 hours.
. Reports are logged with: date received, content reported, reason given, and reporter's contact (if provided).

== Initial assessment

A volunteer with moderation responsibilities reviews the reported content and determines whether it:

* Clearly breaches the code of conduct (act immediately — see below)
* Possibly breaches but requires judgment (escalate to a second volunteer)
* Does not breach (close the report and notify the reporter with a brief explanation)

== Removing content

. If content clearly breaches the code of conduct, a moderating volunteer may remove it immediately without prior approval.
. The person who posted the content is notified of the removal, the reason, and their right to respond.
. The removal is logged.

== Disputed removals

. If the poster disputes the removal, they may submit a written request for review within 7 days.
. A second volunteer (not involved in the original decision) reviews the request.
. If the second volunteer agrees with the removal, it stands. If they disagree, the content is reinstated and the matter is discussed among volunteers.
. If volunteers cannot reach consensus, the matter may be referred to the Custodian Board.

== Repeat or serious breaches

. If a member repeatedly breaches the code of conduct, or commits a single serious breach, the matter is referred to the Custodian Board as a formal complaint.
. The Custodian Board follows the complaint procedure in the bylaws.
