= Custodian
:doctype: article

The Custodian Board is the governing body of Featherston Inc. It exists not to run the Society's day-to-day work, but to protect the community's interests when something goes wrong. Think of it as a jury — it only acts when called upon.

== Selection

Custodians are chosen by *sortition* (random lot) from the pool of eligible Voting Members. This is a deliberate departure from elections. Sortition prevents governance roles from being captured by well-known personalities, organised factions, or those with the resources to campaign. It distributes power broadly and ensures the Board reflects ordinary members rather than the politically motivated.

To be eligible for selection, a member must:

. Be a Voting Member in good standing (having contributed NZ$5 or more in the past 12 months);
. Have consented in writing to serving if selected;
. Not be currently serving a second consecutive term on the Custodian Board; and
. Not be disqualified under the Incorporated Societies Act 2022.

== Term

Each Custodian serves for *one year*, from AGM to AGM. No person may serve more than two consecutive terms. After a gap of at least one year, a former Custodian is again eligible for selection.

== Responsibilities

The Custodian Board is an *oversight body, not a management body*. This distinction is fundamental to the Society's design. Volunteers make all positive decisions — what to build, what to publish, how to organise. The Board cannot direct, override, or veto those decisions.

The Board *may*:

* Investigate formal complaints about volunteer conduct or financial irregularities;
* Mediate disputes between members or volunteers that cannot be resolved directly;
* Dismiss a volunteer who has materially breached the kaupapa or code of conduct, after giving them a fair opportunity to respond;
* Authorise expenditure above NZ$500 (jointly with the Treasurer);
* Call Special General Meetings when required; and
* Appoint the Secretary, Treasurer, and Contact Persons from among Voting Members.

The Board *may not*:

* Direct the content, design, or features of the website;
* Override operational decisions made by volunteers;
* Take any action that concentrates power in one person or group; or
* Make decisions that bind the Society without consensus of all five members.

== Decision-making

All Board decisions require the *consensus of all five members*. There is no casting vote and no simple majority. If consensus cannot be reached at one meeting, the matter is adjourned. If it still cannot be resolved at a subsequent meeting, it is referred to the full membership at a Special General Meeting.

This high bar is intentional. It means the Board can only act when everyone agrees — preventing any subset of the Board from acting unilaterally.

== Meetings

The Board meets *at least once per quarter*, and as often as needed. All five members must be present for a meeting to proceed. Any member may call a meeting with five working days' notice.

At the start of each meeting, the Board appoints a *rotating facilitator* by consensus. This role has no casting vote and no special authority outside of facilitating discussion. It rotates among members.
