= Meeting Facilitator (Chairperson)
:doctype: article

Featherston Inc does not have a permanent Chairperson. This is a deliberate design choice: a fixed chair accumulates informal authority over time and becomes a single point of influence that can be captured or abused.

Instead, the Custodian Board appoints a *rotating facilitator* by consensus at the start of each meeting. Any Board member may serve in this role. It is not a position of seniority — it is a practical function that rotates so that no one person shapes every meeting.

== What the facilitator does

The facilitator's role is to keep the meeting moving fairly and openly. During a meeting, the facilitator:

* Opens the meeting and confirms all five members are present;
* Reads or summarises the agenda;
* Invites each member to speak in turn on each item;
* Keeps discussion on topic and ensures all voices are heard;
* Summarises points of agreement and disagreement clearly;
* Confirms when consensus has been reached; and
* Closes the meeting and confirms any action items.

== What the facilitator does not do

The facilitator does not hold any special decision-making power. Specifically:

* There is *no casting vote*. All decisions require the consensus of all five Board members, regardless of who is facilitating.
* The facilitator cannot rule an item out of order without the agreement of the Board.
* The facilitator's summary or interpretation of consensus can be challenged by any member.
* Outside of a meeting, the facilitator has no role or authority beyond that of any other Board member.

== Why no permanent chair?

Most incorporated societies have a permanent Chairperson who serves for a year or more. The risks are well-documented: the chair shapes agendas, controls discussion, and over time becomes the informal face and authority of the organisation — even when the rules say otherwise.

This constitution removes that risk by making the chair role genuinely temporary: it lasts one meeting, it rotates, and it carries no authority beyond facilitation. Governance authority belongs collectively to all five Custodians equally.

== Legal note

Under section 45 and section 26(1)(f)(ii) of the Incorporated Societies Act 2022, the constitution must address the chairperson and casting vote. This constitution satisfies that requirement by specifying a rotating meeting facilitator with no casting vote and a consensus decision-making model.
